Think about the last time you reached into your medicine cabinet and found only one or two pills left. Did you immediately plan your pharmacy trip? Or did you think "I'll sort it tomorrow," then find yourself out of medication by Thursday evening when the pharmacy is closed?

This scenario plays out millions of times daily across every chronic condition ‚Äî diabetes, hypertension, mental health, thyroid disorders. It's entirely preventable with one simple system: knowing your count before you need to count.

Why "I'll Notice When I'm Running Low" Doesn't Work

The brain is optimistic about future tasks. Seeing 10 pills left feels comfortable because 10 feels like "plenty." By the time the brain registers urgency, you might have 2 days of supply left ‚Äî barely enough time to schedule a doctor's appointment for a renewal, let alone wait for a pharmacy to fill it.

For medications requiring prescriptions, the timeline is even tighter. You may need a doctor's appointment to renew, followed by prescription processing, followed by pharmacy stock and preparation. That's potentially 5-10 days minimum ‚Äî far more than 2 pills' worth of buffer.

How Dozi's Stock Tracking Works

  • Enter Your Starting Count When you add a medication, tell Dozi how many pills you currently have. This takes 5 seconds.
  • Automatic Countdown Every time you confirm a dose in Dozi, your pill count decreases automatically. No manual updating needed.
  • Configurable Warning Threshold Set the number of days' supply at which you want a warning ‚Äî 7 days is our recommended default. You'll receive a notification with enough time to act calmly.
  • Update After Refill Once you pick up your prescription, update your count in Dozi. The cycle continues seamlessly.
